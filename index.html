üìñ Key Vocabulary

Word Meaning
Percentage A number out of 100. 'Per cent' means 'out of 100' in Latin.
% (per cent symbol) The sign we use to show percentages. Example: 50% = 50 out of 100.
Whole The total amount. If there are 100 students, 100 is the whole.
Part A piece of the whole. If 25 students are absent, 25 is the part.

What Does Percentage Mean?

A percentage is a way of showing a part of something out of 100.

üçï Imagine a pizza cut into exactly 100 equal slices:

  • Eat 50 slices, then you ate 50% of the pizza.
  • Eat 25 slices, then you ate 25% of the pizza.
  • Eat 100 slices, then you ate 100% of the pizza.
[ üé® Insert: Colourful Illustration of a Pizza sliced into many pieces ]

In Simple terms....

Percentage = "How many out of 100?"

50% means 50 out of every 100.
10% means 10 out of every 100.
100% means ALL of something ‚Äî the whole thing!

üëÄ Visualise It like this: The 100-Square Grid

Each square below represents 1%. Count the blue squares, and there's your percentage!

35 squares shaded = 35%
65 squares empty = 65%

Together they make 100% (the whole grid)

Let's Work Some Examples

In a class of 100 students, 60 students passed a test. What percentage passed?

Step 1

Identify the part and the whole

Part = 60 students passed. Whole = 100 students total.

Step 2

Write as a fraction over 100

60 out of 100 = 60/100

Step 3

Write using the % symbol

60/100 = 60%

Answer: 60% of the students passed the test.

Chapter 3: Finding a Percentage of a Number

üéØ Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  • Use the percentage formula to find a part of a number

The Percentage Formula

Percentage of a Number =

( % ÷ 100 ) × Whole

Example: 20% of 150

0.20 × 150 = 30

üìå Always: Convert % to decimal FIRST, then multiply!

üß† Worked Example 3A

Find 20% of 150.

Step 1

Convert percentage to decimal

20% = 0.20

Step 2

Multiply by the number

0.20 × 150 = 30

Answer: 20% of 150 = 30

üõçÔ∏è Worked Example 3B

A shirt costs $80. There is a 15% discount. How much is the discount?

Step 1

Convert 15% to decimal

15% = 0.15

Step 2

Multiply by the price

0.15 × $80 = $12

Answer: The discount is $12. You save $12 on the shirt!

‚ö° Quick Mental Maths Shortcuts

Easy Ways to Find Percentages in Your Head
10% of any number → Divide by 10 (10% of 60 = 6)
50% of any number → Divide by 2 (50% of 80 = 40)
25% of any number → Divide by 4 (25% of 200 = 50)
1% of any number → Divide by 100 (1% of 500 = 5)

Chapter 4: Percentage Increase and Decrease

üéØ Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  • Calculate a percentage increase and find the new value
  • Calculate a percentage decrease and find the new value
  • Know the difference between increase and decrease

‚öñÔ∏è Percentage Increase vs Decrease

INCREASE ↑

Original: $200

+ 20% of $200 = $40

New Price: $240

Price went UP by $40

DECREASE ↓

Original: $200

- 20% of $200 = $40

New Price: $160

Price went DOWN by $40

Key Vocabulary

Word Meaning
Increase Getting bigger - the number goes UP.
Decrease Getting smaller - the number goes DOWN.
Original value The number you started with (before any change).
New value The number after the change has happened.

Percentage Increase

Formula

New Value = Original + (Percentage × Original)

Shortcut: New Value = Original × (1 + decimal)

Worked Example 4A - Increase

A phone costs $200. The price increases by 15%. What is the new price?

Step 1

Find 15% of $200

15% = 0.15 → 0.15 × 200 = $30

Step 2

Add the increase to the original

$200 + $30 = $230

Answer: The new price is $230.

Percentage Decrease

Formula

New Value = Original - (Percentage × Original)

Shortcut: New Value = Original × (1 - decimal)

Worked Example 4B - Decrease

A jacket costs $150. It is on sale at 20% off. What is the sale price?

Step 1

Find 20% of $150

20% = 0.20 → 0.20 × 150 = $30

Step 2

Subtract from original price

$150 - $30 = $120

Answer: The sale price is $120. You save $30!

Real Life Story

Amara wants to buy sneakers that cost $90. The store has a "30% OFF" sale. How much does Amara pay?

Step 1 Find the discount amount: 30% of $90 = 0.30 × 90 = $27
Step 2 Subtract the discount: $90 - $27 = $63

Answer: Amara pays $63 for the sneakers.

üçΩÔ∏è Scenario 2: Restaurant Tip

Real Life Story

David's restaurant bill is $60. He wants to leave a 15% tip. How much tip does he leave?

Step 1 Find 15% of $60: 15% = 0.15 → 0.15 × 60 = $9

Answer: David leaves a $9 tip.

Total paid: $60 + $9 = $69

üíØ Scenario 3: Test Score

Real Life Story

Sarah scored 36 out of 40 on her maths test. What is her percentage score?

Step 1 Write as a fraction: 36 out of 40 = 36/40
Step 2 Divide and multiply by 100: (36 ÷ 40) × 100 = 0.9 × 100 = 90%

Answer: Sarah scored 90%! Outstanding! ‚≠ê
